Market context & growth thesis

Rising agency fees plus service expansion create a compounding revenue opportunity.

Over the past few years, we have observed a significant increase in agency management fees across the market — a trend that is also reflected on the Expecting platform. Expecting's management fees have increased by $10,000 over the past three years. We are seeing agencies charging management fees of around $45,000, with some premium or VIP programs reaching as high as $500,000.

In addition to the anticipated increase in our own management fees in line with overall market trends, we expect to drive further revenue growth through additional components of the surrogacy journey. As noted, the agency fee represents only one element of the total program cost. As Expecting expands its service offerings to include legal support, psychological evaluations, financing, and insurance, we will be able to generate additional revenue streams alongside the increase in management fees.

Marketplace perspective

More comprehensive than vertical marketplaces.

Compared with

From a marketplace perspective, Expecting.AI stands out as a more comprehensive platform compared to Tulip Fertility, Nodal, and GoStork. While competitors typically focus on either demand or supply, Expecting.AI operates across both, covering surrogacy, egg, and sperm donation in one unified, AI-driven experience — making it more scalable and product-focused.

Surrogacy agency perspective

Marketplace scale meets agency-grade service.

Compared with
Circle Surrogacy ConceiveAbilities Growing Generations Hatch Fertility

From a surrogacy agency perspective, Expecting.AI competes with Circle Surrogacy, ConceiveAbilities, Growing Generations, and Hatch Fertility. These agencies provide high-touch services but operate closed networks and slower matching cycles. Expecting.AI differentiates itself by combining agency services with marketplace scale — enabling faster, more flexible matching through its broad provider network and data-driven approach.
